Dissertation

Why do people protest? In my dissertation, I address this question from three perspectives: Post-materialism, collective action, and strategic interaction between the government and civil society. Motivated by the recent Anti-Extradition Law Movement, I examine the underlying factors that drive ordinary Hong Kong people to protest. I also explore how information and beliefs influence the actions of the government and protesters. My dissertation committee includes Tse-min Lin (Chair), Robert Luskin, Brian Roberts, and Cara J Wong.
